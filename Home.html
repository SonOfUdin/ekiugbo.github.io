Welcome to my webpage. I am a Faculty member at the National Institute for Nigerian Languages, Aba, Nigeria where I teach courses in Linguistics and English Language. My current research interests includes: Phonology, Sociolinguistics, Computational linguistics, Ethnography and Language documentation. I hold a BA and MA degrees in Linguistics from Delta State University, Abraka, and the University of Benin respectively. I am currently a PhD (Linguistics) candidate at Nnamdi Azikiwe University, Awka.

LANGUAGE DOCUMENTATION PROJECTS

  • Erohwa Orthography & Lexical Database [Sole investigator, with funding from the Foundation for Endangered Languages, 2018]: The aim of the project was to develop a writing system for Erohwa and produced a database of lexical materials.

  • Collection & Documentation of Olomu Oral Traditions [Sole investigator, with funding from the Firebird Foundation for Anthropological Research, 2018]: The project sought to collect for documentation purposes, the folktales (especially moon light tale), dirges, riddles, proverbs, important historical events, native festivals, war chants and ritual language. These aspects of the oral literature and traditional ecological knowledge of the Urhobo people of Olomu are severely endangered. The endangerment results from the strong influence of globalization on the cultural practices of the people, which endangers all aspects of the language and the culture of the people.

  • Urhobo Reader Project [Research Assistant to Dr. (Mrs.) M. E. Ajiboye, with funding from the Foundation for Endangered Languages, 2020]: The project aims to develop an Urhobo reading material for higher basic school (JS 1-3) to allow for the proper teaching and testing of the language.

  • A Dictionary of Urhobo Given Name [Sole-Investigator, Self-Funded, 2019-Date]: The project aims to document the given names of Urhobo origin and their meaning
